"A notebook of length \(15 ~cm\) was measured to be \(16.8 ~cm\), calculate, correct to two decimal places, the percentage error in the measurement.
Possible answers:
A. \(12.00\%\)
B. \(11.71\%\)
C. \(10.71\%\)
D. \(11.21\%\)
Correct answer: A"

Solution.

To calculate the percentage error in the measurement of the notebook's length, we use the formula:
\[ \\ \text{Percentage Error} = \left( \frac{|\text{Measured Value} - \text{Actual Value}|}{\text{Actual Value}} \right) \times 100 \\ \]
Given:
- Actual length = 15 cm
- Measured length = 16.8 cm
First, find the absolute error:
\[ \\ |16.8 - 15| = 1.8 \text{ cm} \\ \]
Next, divide the absolute error by the actual value:
\[ \\ \frac{1.8}{15} = 0.12 \\ \]
Convert this to a percentage by multiplying by 100:
\[ \\ 0.12 \times 100 = 12.00\% \\ \]
Thus, the percentage error, correct to two decimal places, is 12.00%. Therefore, the correct answer is:
\[ \\ \boxed{A} \\ \]
