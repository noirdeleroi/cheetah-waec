"One-third of the sum of two numbers is \(12\), twice their difference is \(12\). Find the numbers.
Possible answers:
A. \(22\) and \(14\)
B. \(20\) and \(16\)
C. \(21\) and \(15\)
D. \(23\) and \(13\)
Correct answer: C"

Solution.

Let the two numbers be \( x \) and \( y \).
The first condition states that one-third of the sum of the two numbers is 12:
\[ \\ \frac{1}{3}(x + y) = 12 \\ \]
Multiplying both sides by 3, we get:
\[ \\ x + y = 36 \\ \]
The second condition states that twice their difference is 12:
\[ \\ 2(x - y) = 12 \\ \]
Dividing both sides by 2, we get:
\[ \\ x - y = 6 \\ \]
We now have the system of equations:
1. \( x + y = 36 \)
2. \( x - y = 6 \)
Adding these two equations:
\[ \\ (x + y) + (x - y) = 36 + 6 \\ \]
\[ \\ 2x = 42 \\ \]
\[ \\ x = 21 \\ \]
Substituting \( x = 21 \) back into the first equation:
\[ \\ 21 + y = 36 \\ \]
\[ \\ y = 15 \\ \]
Thus, the numbers are 21 and 15. Verifying the conditions:
- The sum is \( 21 + 15 = 36 \), and one-third of 36 is 12.
- The difference is \( 21 - 15 = 6 \), and twice 6 is 12.
Both conditions are satisfied, so the answer is \(\boxed{C}\).
