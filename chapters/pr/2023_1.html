"In the diagram, \(O\) is the center of the circle and \(\angle \mathrm{SQR}=28^{\circ}\). Find \(\angle \mathrm{ORS}\).
Possible answers:
A. \(56^{\circ}\)
B. \(28^{\circ}\)
C. \(76^{\circ}\)
D. \(62^{\circ}\)
Correct answer: D"

Solution.

Given the diagram where \(O\) is the center of the circle and \(\angle \mathrm{SQR} = 28^\circ\), we need to find \(\angle \mathrm{ORS}\).
1. **Inscribed Angle Theorem**: The inscribed angle \(\angle \mathrm{SQR}\) intercepts arc \(\mathrm{SR}\). According to the theorem, the measure of the inscribed angle is half the measure of its intercepted arc. Therefore, the measure of arc \(\mathrm{SR}\) is \(2 \times 28^\circ = 56^\circ\).
2. **Central Angle**: The central angle subtended by arc \(\mathrm{SR}\) is \(\angle \mathrm{SOR}\). Since the central angle is equal to the measure of its arc, \(\angle \mathrm{SOR} = 56^\circ\).
3. **Isosceles Triangle**: In triangle \(\mathrm{SOR}\), sides \(\mathrm{OS}\) and \(\mathrm{OR}\) are radii of the circle, making it isosceles. The base angles \(\angle \mathrm{OSR}\) and \(\angle \mathrm{ORS}\) are equal. The sum of the angles in a triangle is \(180^\circ\), so:
\[ \\ \angle \mathrm{OSR} + \angle \mathrm{ORS} + \angle \mathrm{SOR} = 180^\circ \\ \]
Substituting the known values:
\[ \\ \angle \mathrm{OSR} + \angle \mathrm{ORS} + 56^\circ = 180^\circ \\ \]
Since \(\angle \mathrm{OSR} = \angle \mathrm{ORS}\):
\[ \\ 2 \times \angle \mathrm{ORS} + 56^\circ = 180^\circ \\ \]
Solving for \(\angle \mathrm{ORS}\):
\[ \\ 2 \times \angle \mathrm{ORS} = 124^\circ \\ \]
\[ \\ \angle \mathrm{ORS} = 62^\circ \\ \]
Thus, the measure of \(\angle \mathrm{ORS}\) is \(\boxed{D}\).
