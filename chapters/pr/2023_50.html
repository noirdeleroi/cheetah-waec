"Find the mean deviation of numbers: \(14, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19\).
Possible answers:
A. \(2.5\)
B. \(1.7\)
C. \(1.5\)
D. \(3.5\)
Correct answer: C"

Solution.

To find the mean deviation of the numbers \(14, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19\):
1. **Calculate the mean**:
\[ \\ \text{Mean} = \frac{14 + 15 + 16 + 17 + 18 + 19}{6} = \frac{99}{6} = 16.5 \\ \]
2. **Find the absolute deviations from the mean**:
\[ \\ \begin{align*} \\ |14 - 16.5| &= 2.5, \\ \\ |15 - 16.5| &= 1.5, \\ \\ |16 - 16.5| &= 0.5, \\ \\ |17 - 16.5| &= 0.5, \\ \\ |18 - 16.5| &= 1.5, \\ \\ |19 - 16.5| &= 2.5. \\ \end{align*} \\ \]
3. **Calculate the mean of these absolute deviations**:
\[ \\ \text{Mean Deviation} = \frac{2.5 + 1.5 + 0.5 + 0.5 + 1.5 + 2.5}{6} = \frac{9}{6} = 1.5 \\ \]
Thus, the mean deviation is \(\boxed{C}\).
