"A. Complete the table of values of \(\mathrm{y}=2 x^2-x-4\) for \(-3 \leq \mathrm{x} \leq 3\), write the answer as a sequence of missing values for \(y\) coresponding to the values of \(x\) from \(-2\) to \(3\).
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|l|l|l|l|l|}\hline \(\mathrm{x}\) & -3 & -2 & -1 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 3
\hline y & 17 & & & -4 & & &
\hline\end{tabular}
B. Using a scale of \(2 ~cm\) to \(1\) unit on the \(x\)-axis and \(2 ~cm\) to \(2\) unit on the \(y\)-axis, draw the graph of \(\mathrm{y}=2 x^2-x-4\) for \(-3 \leq \mathrm{x} \leq 3\).
C. i. Use the graph to find: the roots of the equation \(2 x^2-x-4\).
ii. Use the graph to find the: values of \(x\) for which \(y\) increases as \(x\) increases. Represent the asnwer as \(x > \frac{a}{b}\), where \(a,b\) are integers, write down the answer as \(a,b\).
iii. Use the graph to find, correct to one decimal place, the minimum point of \(y\).\"

Correct answer:
6,-1,-3,2,11
Correct answer:
graph
Correct answer:
-1.2::1.7
Correct answer:
1,4
Correct answer:
-4.1"

Solution.

A. The missing y values for \( x = -2, -1, 1, 2, 3 \) are calculated as follows:
- For \( x = -2 \): \( y = 2(-2)^2 - (-2) - 4 = 6 \)
- For \( x = -1 \): \( y = 2(-1)^2 - (-1) - 4 = -1 \)
- For \( x = 1 \): \( y = 2(1)^2 - 1 - 4 = -3 \)
- For \( x = 2 \): \( y = 2(2)^2 - 2 - 4 = 2 \)
- For \( x = 3 \): \( y = 2(3)^2 - 3 - 4 = 11 \)
**Answer:** \( 6, -1, -3, 2, 11 \)
B. For the graph we use y values for \( x = -3, -2, -1, 1, 2, 3 \). Value for \(-3\) is \( y = 2(-3)^2 - (-3) - 4 = 17 \). Other values were computed in Part A.
Scaling:
x-axis: 2 \, cm = 1 \ unit,
y-axis: 2 \, cm = 2 \, units (equivalent to 1 \, cm = 1 \, unit).

C.
i. The roots of the equation \( 2x^2 - x - 4 = 0 \) are approximately where the graph crosses the x-axis. Using the quadratic formula, the exact roots are \( \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{33}}{4} \approx -1.2 \) and \( 1.7 \).
**Answer:** \( x \approx -1.2 \) and \( x \approx 1.7 \)
ii. The function increases for \( x > \frac{1}{4} \).
**Answer:** \( 1, 4 \)
iii. The minimum point occurs at the vertex \( \left( \frac{1}{4}, -\frac{33}{8} \right) \).
**Answer:** \( \left( \frac{1}{4}, -\frac{33}{8} \right) \)
### Final Answer
A. \(\boxed{6}\), \(\boxed{-1}\), \(\boxed{-3}\), \(\boxed{2}\), \(\boxed{11}\)
C.i. Roots: \(\boxed{-1.2}\) and \(\boxed{1.7}\)
C.ii. \(\boxed{1}\), \(\boxed{4}\)
C.iii. Minimum point: \(\boxed{\left( \frac{1}{4}, -\frac{33}{8} \right)}\)
