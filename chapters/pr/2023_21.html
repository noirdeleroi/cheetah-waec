"If \(\mathrm{m}: \mathrm{n}=2 \frac{1}{3}: 1 \frac{1}{5}\) and \(\mathrm{n}: \mathrm{q}=1 \frac{1}{2}: 1 \frac{1}{3}\), find \(\mathrm{q}: \mathrm{m}\).
Possible answers:
A. \(35:18\)
B. \(16:35\)
C. \(18:35\)
D. \(35:16\)
Correct answer: B"

Solution.

First, we convert the mixed numbers in the given ratios to improper fractions:
1. For the ratio \( \mathrm{m} : \mathrm{n} = 2 \frac{1}{3} : 1 \frac{1}{5} \):
- \( 2 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{3} \)
- \( 1 \frac{1}{5} = \frac{6}{5} \)
- The ratio becomes \( \frac{7}{3} : \frac{6}{5} \)
- Simplifying, \( \frac{7}{3} \div \frac{6}{5} = \frac{7}{3} \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{35}{18} \)
- Therefore, \( \mathrm{m} : \mathrm{n} = 35 : 18 \)
2. For the ratio \( \mathrm{n} : \mathrm{q} = 1 \frac{1}{2} : 1 \frac{1}{3} \):
- \( 1 \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2} \)
- \( 1 \frac{1}{3} = \frac{4}{3} \)
- The ratio becomes \( \frac{3}{2} : \frac{4}{3} \)
- Simplifying, \( \frac{3}{2} \div \frac{4}{3} = \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{9}{8} \)
- Therefore, \( \mathrm{n} : \mathrm{q} = 9 : 8 \)
Next, we combine the two ratios by making the common term \( \mathrm{n} \) the same:
- The first ratio \( \mathrm{m} : \mathrm{n} = 35 : 18 \)
- The second ratio \( \mathrm{n} : \mathrm{q} = 9 : 8 \) is scaled to \( 18 : 16 \) by multiplying both parts by 2
Combining these, we get the ratio \( \mathrm{m} : \mathrm{n} : \mathrm{q} = 35 : 18 : 16 \). Therefore, the ratio \( \mathrm{q} : \mathrm{m} \) is \( 16 : 35 \).
The final answer is \(\boxed{B}\).
