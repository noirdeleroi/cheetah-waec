"In the diagram, \(N R\) is a diameter, \(\angle \mathrm{MNR}=\mathrm{x}^{\circ}\) and, \(\angle \mathrm{SRN}=(5 \mathrm{x}+20)^{\circ}\). Find the value of \(2x^{\circ}\).
Possible answers:
A. \(42^{\circ}\)
B. \(35^{\circ}\)
C. \(20^{\circ}\)
D. \(90^{\circ}\)
Correct answer: B"

Solution.

Given the diagram where \(NR\) is a diameter of the circle with center \(O\), \(\angle MNR = x^\circ\), and \(\angle SRN = (5x + 20)^\circ\), we need to find the value of \(2x^\circ\).
1. **Thales' Theorem**: Since \(NR\) is the diameter, \(\angle NMR = 90^\circ\) (right angle at \(M\)).
2. **Triangle MNR**: In triangle \(MNR\), the sum of angles is \(180^\circ\). Therefore, \(\angle MRN = 90^\circ - x^\circ\).
3. **External Angle Theorem**: The external angle at \(R\) (i.e., \(\angle SRN\)) is equal to the sum of the non-adjacent internal angles of triangle \(MNR\). Thus, \(\angle SRN = \angle NMR + \angle MNR = 90^\circ + x^\circ\).
4. **Setting Up the Equation**: Given \(\angle SRN = (5x + 20)^\circ\), we equate this to the external angle:
\[ \\ 5x + 20 = 90 + x \\ \]
5. **Solving for \(x\)**:
\[ \\ 5x + 20 = 90 + x, \\ \\ 4x = 70, \\ \\ x = 17.5 \\ \]
6. **Finding \(2x\)**:
\[ \\ 2x = 2 \times 17.5 = 35 \\ \]
Thus, the value of \(2x^\circ\) is \(\boxed{B}\).
