"If \(2 x-3 y=-11\) and \(3 x+2 y=3\), evaluate \((y-x)^2\).
Possible answers:
A. \(16\)
B. \(25\)
C. \(9\)
D. \(4\)
Correct answer: A"

Solution.

To solve the system of equations \(2x - 3y = -11\) and \(3x + 2y = 3\), we use the elimination method.
First, we multiply the equations to align the coefficients of \(x\):
1. Multiply the first equation by 3: \(6x - 9y = -33\)
2. Multiply the second equation by 2: \(6x + 4y = 6\)
Subtract the first modified equation from the second modified equation:
\[ \\ (6x + 4y) - (6x - 9y) = 6 - (-33) \\ \]
Simplifying the left side:
\[ \\ 6x + 4y - 6x + 9y = 13y \\ \]
Simplifying the right side:
\[ \\ 6 + 33 = 39 \\ \]
This gives:
\[ \\ 13y = 39 \implies y = 3 \\ \]
Substitute \(y = 3\) back into the second original equation \(3x + 2y = 3\):
\[ \\ 3x + 2(3) = 3 \implies 3x + 6 = 3 \implies 3x = -3 \implies x = -1 \\ \]
Thus, the solutions are \(x = -1\) and \(y = 3\). Evaluating \((y - x)^2\):
\[ \\ (3 - (-1))^2 = (3 + 1)^2 = 4^2 = 16 \\ \]
The final answer is \(\boxed{A}\).
