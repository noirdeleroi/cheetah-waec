"\(M\) varies jointly as the square of \(n\) and square root of \(q\). If \(M=24\) when \(n=2\) and \(q=4\), find \(M\) when \(n=5, q=9\).
Possible answers:
A. \(288\)
B. \(400\)
C. \(300\)
D. \(225\)
Correct answer: D"

Solution.

Given that \( M \) varies jointly as the square of \( n \) and the square root of \( q \), we can express this relationship as:
\[ M = k \cdot n^2 \cdot \sqrt{q} \]
where \( k \) is the constant of proportionality.
We are given that \( M = 24 \) when \( n = 2 \) and \( q = 4 \). Substituting these values into the equation to find \( k \):
\[ 24 = k \cdot (2)^2 \cdot \sqrt{4} \]
\[ 24 = k \cdot 4 \cdot 2 \]
\[ 24 = 8k \]
\[ k = \frac{24}{8} = 3 \]
Now that we have determined \( k = 3 \), the equation becomes:
\[ M = 3 \cdot n^2 \cdot \sqrt{q} \]
We need to find \( M \) when \( n = 5 \) and \( q = 9 \). Substituting these values into the equation:
\[ M = 3 \cdot (5)^2 \cdot \sqrt{9} \]
\[ M = 3 \cdot 25 \cdot 3 \]
\[ M = 3 \cdot 75 \]
\[ M = 225 \]
Thus, the final answer is \(\boxed{D}\).
