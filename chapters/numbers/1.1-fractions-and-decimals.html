1.1 Fractions and Decimals

Fractions and decimals are fundamental concepts in arithmetic. Operations with fractions include addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Fractions can also be simplified to their lowest terms by determining the greatest common factor (GCF) of the numerator and denominator.

Decimals are numbers written in a base-10 system and can be converted to fractions and vice versa.

Key Concepts:

  • Least Common Multiple (LCM): The smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.
  • Highest Common Factor (HCF): The largest number that divides two or more numbers without leaving a remainder.
  • Factorization: Breaking a number into its prime factors.
  • Addition and Subtraction of Fractions:
    \[ \frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad + bc}{bd}, \quad \frac{a}{b} - \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad - bc}{bd} \]
  • Multiplication of Fractions:
    \[ \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ac}{bd} \]
  • Division of Fractions:
    \[ \frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c} = \frac{ad}{bc} \]
  • Mixed Numbers: Convert to improper fractions:
    \[ a\frac{b}{c} = \frac{ac + b}{c} \]

Example 1: Addition of Fractions

\[ \frac{3}{4} + \frac{5}{6} = \frac{9}{12} + \frac{10}{12} = \frac{19}{12} \]

Example 2: Multiplication of Fractions

\[ \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5} = \frac{8}{15} \]

Example 3: Division of Fractions

\[ \frac{5}{6} \div \frac{2}{9} = \frac{5}{6} \times \frac{9}{2} = \frac{45}{12} = \frac{15}{4} \]

Example 4: Multiplication of Decimals

\[ 1.25 \times 0.4 = 0.5 \]

Example 5: Factorization of 84

\[ 84 = 2^2 \times 3 \times 7 \]

Example 6: HCF of 48 and 180

\[ 48 = 2^4 \times 3, \quad 180 = 2^2 \times 3^2 \times 5 \Rightarrow \text{HCF} = 2^2 \times 3 = 12 \]

Example 7: Expression with Mixed Numbers

\[ \frac{1\frac{7}{8} \times 2\frac{2}{5}}{6\frac{3}{4} \div \frac{3}{4}} = \frac{\frac{15}{8} \times \frac{12}{5}}{\frac{27}{4} \div \frac{3}{4}} = \frac{\frac{180}{40}}{\frac{108}{12}} = \frac{\frac{9}{2}}{9} = \frac{1}{2} \]
