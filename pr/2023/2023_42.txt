"The length of the diagonal of a square is $12 ~cm$. Calculate the area of the square. \\ Possible answers: \\ A. $36 \mathrm{~cm}^2$ \\ B. $48 \mathrm{~cm}^2$ \\ C. $72 \mathrm{~cm}^2$ \\ D. $18 \mathrm{~cm}^2$ \\ Correct answer: C" \\ \textbf{Solution.} To calculate the area of the square when given the diagonal length of 12 cm, follow these steps: \\ 1. **Relate diagonal to side length**: \\ For a square with side length \( a \), the diagonal \( d \) is \( d = a\sqrt{2} \). \\ Given \( d = 12 \), solve for \( a \): \\ \[ \\ a = \frac{12}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{12\sqrt{2}}{2} = 6\sqrt{2} \, \text{cm}. \\ \] \\ 2. **Calculate the area**: \\ The area \( A \) of the square is \( a^2 \): \\ \[ \\ A = (6\sqrt{2})^2 = 6^2 \cdot (\sqrt{2})^2 = 36 \cdot 2 = 72 \, \text{cm}^2. \\ \] \\ \begin{figure}[ht] \\ \centering \\ \includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{/home/alex/Downloads/pics_waec/latex_code_solutions/2023_S_42.png} \\ \caption{} \\ \label{fig:1} \\ \end{figure} \\ **Alternative method**: \\ Use the direct formula for the area in terms of the diagonal: \\ \[ \\ A = \frac{d^2}{2} = \frac{12^2}{2} = \frac{144}{2} = 72 \, \text{cm}^2. \\ \] \\ **Answer**: C. \( 72 \, \mathrm{~cm}^2 \)
