"If $\log _a 3=m$ and $\log _a 5=p$, find $\log _a 75$. \\ Possible answers: \\ A. $m^{2}+p$ \\ B. $2m+p$ \\ C. $m+2p$ \\ D. $m+p^{2}$ \\ Correct answer: C" \\ \textbf{Solution.} To find \(\log_a 75\) given \(\log_a 3 = m\) and \(\log_a 5 = p\): \\ 1. **Factorize 75**: \\ \(75 = 3 \times 5^2\). \\ 2. **Apply logarithm product rule**: \\ \[ \\ \log_a 75 = \log_a (3 \times 5^2) = \log_a 3 + \log_a 5^2 \\ \] \\ 3. **Use the power rule for \(\log_a 5^2\)**: \\ \[ \\ \log_a 5^2 = 2 \log_a 5 = 2p \\ \] \\ 4. **Substitute given values**: \\ \[ \\ \log_a 75 = \log_a 3 + 2p = m + 2p \\ \] \\ **Answer**: \(\boxed{C}\)
