"a. In a town, Chief $X$ resides $60 ~m$ away on a bearing of $057^{\circ}$ from Palace $P$, while Chief $Y$ resides on a bearing of $150^{\circ}$ from the same Palace $P$. The residence of $X$ and $Y$ are $180 ~m$ apart. Illustrate the information in a diagram. \\ b. Find and correct to three significant figures, the: i. bearing of $X$ from $Y$; ii. distance between $P$ and $Y$.\" \\ \\ Correct answer: \\ graph\\ Correct answer: \\  349\\ Correct answer: \\  167" \\ \textbf{Solution.} \section*{Bearings Problem via Sine \& Cosine Laws} \\ \noindent \\ \textbf{Given:} \\ \begin{itemize} \\ \item Palace $P$ is our reference point. \\ \item Chief $X$ is $60\,\mathrm{m}$ from $P$ on a bearing of $057^\circ$. \\ \item Chief $Y$ is on a bearing of $150^\circ$ from $P,$ at unknown distance $PY=d.$ \\ \item The distance $XY$ between $X$ and $Y$ is $180\,\mathrm{m}.$ \\ \end{itemize} \\ From the bearing angles $057^\circ$ and $150^\circ,$ the angle at $P$ in triangle $PXY$ is \\ \[ \\ \angle P \;=\; 150^\circ \;-\;57^\circ \;=\;93^\circ. \\ \] \\ Label the sides so that $PX=60$ is opposite $\angle Y,$ $PY=d$ is opposite $\angle X,$ and $XY=180$ is opposite $\angle P.$ \\ \medskip \\ \textbf{(a)~Diagram.} \\ A sketch of $\triangle PXY$ with: \\ \[ \\ \begin{aligned} \\ PX &= 60,\quad XY = 180,\quad PY=d,\\ \\ \angle P&=93^\circ,\quad \angle X + \angle Y + 93^\circ=180^\circ. \\ \end{aligned} \\ \] \\ Bearing lines from $P$ make $57^\circ$ to $PX$ and $150^\circ$ to $PY.$ \\ \subsection*{(b\,i)~Bearing of $X$ from $Y$ (Law of Sines)} \\ First, we find the \emph{interior} angle at $Y$ in $\triangle PXY.$  By the Sine Rule, \\ \[ \\ \frac{\sin(\angle Y)}{PX} \\ \;=\; \\ \frac{\sin(\angle P)}{XY} \\ \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; \\ \sin(\angle Y) \\ \;=\; \\ \frac{PX}{XY}\,\sin(\angle P) \\ \;=\; \\ \frac{60}{180}\,\sin(93^\circ). \\ \] \\ Numerically, \\ \[ \\ \sin(93^\circ)\approx 0.9945, \\ \quad \\ \sin(\angle Y) \\ \approx \\ \frac{60}{180}\times 0.9945 \\ = 0.3315, \\ \] \\ \[ \\ \angle Y \\ \;=\;\sin^{-1}(0.3315) \\ \;\approx\;19.3^\circ. \\ \] \\ Next, recall that from $Y$'s point of view, the line $YP$ has \emph{reverse} bearing $150^\circ + 180^\circ = 330^\circ.$ \\ Inside the triangle, moving from $YP$ to $YX$ sweeps out $\angle Y \approx 19.3^\circ.$ \\ Hence the bearing of $X$ \emph{from} $Y$ is \\ \[ \\ \boxed{\,330^\circ \;+\;19.3^\circ \;=\;349^\circ\,}\quad\text{(to 3 s.f.).} \\ \] \\ \begin{figure}[ht] \\ \centering \\ \includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{/home/alex/Downloads/pics_waec/latex_code_solutions/2023_S_60.png} \\ \caption{} \\ \label{fig:1} \\ \end{figure} \\ \subsection*{(b\,ii)~Distance $PY$ (Law of Cosines)} \\ We now find $d=PY.$  By the Cosine Rule on side $XY=180$ opposite $\angle P=93^\circ,$ \\ \[ \\ XY^2 \\ \;=\; \\ PX^2 + PY^2 \;-\;2\,(PX)(PY)\,\cos(\angle P). \\ \] \\ Hence \\ \[ \\ 180^2 \\ = \\ 60^2 + d^2 \\ -\,2\times 60\times d\,\cos(93^\circ). \\ \] \\ That is \\ \[ \\ 32400 \\ \;=\; \\ 3600 + d^2 \;-\;120\,d\,\cos(93^\circ). \\ \] \\ Since $\cos(93^\circ)\approx -0.05234,$ the equation becomes \\ \[ \\ 32400 \\ = \\ 3600 + d^2 + (120\,d)\times 0.05234, \\ \] \\ leading to a quadratic in $d.$  Solving yields \\ \[ \\ d \;\approx\;166.6\,\mathrm{m}. \\ \] \\ Rounded to three significant figures, \\ \[ \\ \boxed{\,PY\;\approx\;167\,\mathrm{m}\,}. \\ \] \\ \bigskip \\ \textbf{Final Answers (3 s.f.)}: \\ \begin{enumerate} \\ \item[(i)] Bearing of $X$ from $Y$ $\;\approx\; 349^\circ.$ \\ \item[(ii)] Distance $P$--$Y$ $\;\approx\; 167\,\mathrm{m}.$ \\ \end{enumerate}
