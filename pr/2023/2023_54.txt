Thus, \(\angle SRQ = 100^\circ\), which is an inscribed angle subtending major arc \(SQ = 200^\circ\). \\ - The central angle for arc \(RQ\) is \(80^\circ\) (from triangle \(KRQ\)), so arc \(RQ = 80^\circ\). \\ - Arc \(QP\) is calculated as \(360^\circ - 80^\circ - 98^\circ - 80^\circ = 102^\circ\). \\ - Arc \(RP\) is the sum of arcs \(RQ\) and \(QP\), which is \(80^\circ + 102^\circ = 182^\circ\). The minor arc \(RP\) is \(360^\circ - 182^\circ = 178^\circ\). \\ - \(\angle RQP\) is an inscribed angle subtending minor arc \(RP\), so \(\angle RQP = \frac{1}{2} \times 178^\circ = 89^\circ\). \\ \begin{figure}[ht] \\ \centering \\ \includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{/home/alex/Downloads/pics_waec/latex_code_solutions/2023_S_54.png} \\ \caption{} \\ \label{fig:1} \\ \end{figure} \\ 2. **Finding \(\angle SPQ\):** \\ - In cyclic quadrilateral \(SPQR\), opposite angles sum to \(180^\circ\). Given \(\angle SRQ = 100^\circ\), \(\angle SPQ = 180^\circ - 100^\circ = 80^\circ\). \\ Thus, the final answers are: \\ \[ \\ \text{A. } \boxed{89^\circ} \\ \] \\ \[ \\ \text{B. } \boxed{80^\circ} \\ \]
