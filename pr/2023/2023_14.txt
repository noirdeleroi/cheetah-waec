"Solve $2^{5 x} \div 2^x=\sqrt[5]{2^{10}}$. \\ Possible answers: \\ A. $\frac{3}{2}$ \\ B. $\frac{1}{2}$ \\ C. $\frac{1}{3}$ \\ D. $\frac{5}{3}$ \\ Correct answer: B" \\ \textbf{Solution.} To solve the equation \(2^{5x} \div 2^x = \sqrt[5]{2^{10}}\), follow these steps: \\ 1. **Simplify the left side** using exponent division rules: \\ \(2^{5x} \div 2^x = 2^{5x - x} = 2^{4x}\). \\ 2. **Simplify the right side** by converting the fifth root to an exponent: \\ \(\sqrt[5]{2^{10}} = (2^{10})^{\frac{1}{5}} = 2^{10 \cdot \frac{1}{5}} = 2^2 = 4\). \\ 3. **Set the exponents equal** since the bases are the same: \\ \(4x = 2\). \\ 4. **Solve for \(x\)**: \\ \(x = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}\). \\ **Verification**: Substitute \(x = \frac{1}{2}\) back into the original equation: \\ - Left side: \(2^{5 \cdot \frac{1}{2}} \div 2^{\frac{1}{2}} = 2^{\frac{5}{2} - \frac{1}{2}} = 2^2 = 4\). \\ - Right side: \(\sqrt[5]{2^{10}} = 4\). \\ Both sides equal \(4\), confirming the solution is correct. \\ **Answer**: \(\boxed{B}\)
