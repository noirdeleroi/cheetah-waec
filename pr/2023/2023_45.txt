"Find the gradient of the line passing through the points $(\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{3})$ and $(3, \frac{2}{3})$. \\ Possible answers: \\ A. $\frac{2}{5}$ \\ B. $\frac{5}{2}$ \\ C. $\frac{2}{7}$ \\ D. $\frac{7}{2}$ \\ Correct answer: A" \\ \textbf{Solution.} To find the gradient of the line passing through the points \(\left(\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{3}\right)\) and \(\left(3, \frac{2}{3}\right)\), we use the formula for the gradient \(m\) between two points \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\): \\ \[ \\ m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \\ \] \\ Labeling the points, we have \((x_1, y_1) = \left(\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{3}\right)\) and \((x_2, y_2) = \left(3, \frac{2}{3}\right)\). \\ First, calculate the change in \(y\): \\ \[ \\ y_2 - y_1 = \frac{2}{3} - \left(-\frac{1}{3}\right) = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3}{3} = 1 \\ \] \\ Next, calculate the change in \(x\): \\ \[ \\ x_2 - x_1 = 3 - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{6}{2} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{2} \\ \] \\ Now, divide the change in \(y\) by the change in \(x\): \\ \[ \\ m = \frac{1}{\frac{5}{2}} = 1 \times \frac{2}{5} = \frac{2}{5} \\ \] \\ Thus, the gradient of the line is \(\boxed{A}\).
