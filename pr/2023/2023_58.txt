"A. Complete the table of values of $\mathrm{y}=2 x^2-x-4$ for $-3 \leq \mathrm{x} \leq 3$, write the answer as a sequence of missing values for $y$ coresponding to the values of $x$ from $-2$ to $3$. \\ \begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|l|l|l|l|l|}\hline $\mathrm{x}$ & -3 & -2 & -1 & 0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\\hline y & 17 & & & -4 & & & \\\hline\end{tabular} \\ B. The missing y values for \( x = -2, -1, 1, 2, 3 \) are calculated as follows: \\ - For \( x = -2 \): \( y = 2(-2)^2 - (-2) - 4 = 6 \) \\ - For \( x = -1 \): \( y = 2(-1)^2 - (-1) - 4 = -1 \) \\ - For \( x = 1 \): \( y = 2(1)^2 - 1 - 4 = -3 \) \\ - For \( x = 2 \): \( y = 2(2)^2 - 2 - 4 = 2 \) \\ - For \( x = 3 \): \( y = 2(3)^2 - 3 - 4 = 11 \) \\ **Answer:** \( 6, -1, -3, 2, 11 \) \\ B. For the graph we use y values for \( x = -3, -2, -1, 1, 2, 3 \). Value for $-3$ is \( y = 2(-3)^2 - (-3) - 4 = 17 \).
